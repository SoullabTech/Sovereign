12 Facets of Spiralogic Profile  
#12facets #spiralogic #learningstyles 
1. Self-Awareness- Ego, Persona, and Vision for the Future
2. Self-In-World Awareness- Personal expression and Self/World Resonance
3. Transcendent Self-Awareness- Awareness of One’s spiritual essence and path

  

4. Emotional Intelligence- The capacity to feel seen, nurtured, and at home in the world.
5. Personal, Inner Transformation- Transforming out-moded subconscious patterns into redeemed, resonant, and coherent patterns of being.
6. Deep, Internal Self-Awareness- Connection with a deep sense of Self, one’s inner gold or soul.
7. Purpose, Mission, and Service- Developing an awareness of one’s place in the world, where we give back to humanity and the planet.
8. Resources, Plans, and Outer Development- Bringing together plans, teams, and all needed resources to be successful in the world.
9. Well-Formed Plan of Action, Code of Conduct, Ethics, and Service- A refined plan of action, roadmap, and medicine to offer the world. 
10. Interpersonal Relationship Patterns- Perfecting ways of relating to others one-to-one.
11. Collective and Collaborative Dynamics- Perfecting ways of relating to groups, family, and the collective world.
12. Codified Systems and Productive Communications- Developing proficiency in communicating, especially within codified systems and formal situations/institutions.

  

12 Learning Styles based on the 12 facets above

  

1. Ego-centric Learning: Focusing on developing self-awareness through understanding one's ego, persona, and vision for the future.  
      
    
2. Self-expression Learning: Focusing on developing self-awareness through personal expression and exploring the connection between the self and the world.  
      
    
3. Spiritual Learning: Focusing on developing self-awareness through exploring one's spiritual essence and path.  
      
    
4. Emotional Intelligence Learning: Focusing on developing the capacity to feel seen, nurtured, and at home in the world through emotional intelligence.  
      
    
5. Inner Transformation Learning: Focusing on transforming subconscious patterns into redeemed, resonant, and coherent patterns of being.  
      
    
6. Deep Self-awareness Learning: Focusing on connecting with a deep sense of self, one's inner gold or soul.  
      
    
7. Purpose-driven Learning: Focusing on developing an awareness of one's place in the world, including how to give back to humanity and the planet.  
      
    
8. Resourceful Learning: Focusing on bringing together plans, teams, and all needed resources to be successful in the world.  
      
    
9. Ethical Learning: Focusing on refining one's plan of action, roadmap, and medicine to offer the world, with a strong emphasis on code of conduct, ethics, and service.  
      
    
10. Interpersonal Learning: Focusing on perfecting ways of relating to others one-to-one.  
      
    
11. Collaborative Learning: Focusing on perfecting ways of relating to groups, family, and the collective world.  
      
    
12. Communication Learning: Focusing on developing proficiency in communicating, especially within codified systems and formal situations/institutions.  
      
    

Quiz with a 5 point scale rating:

1. When learning something new, how important is it to you that it relates to your personal beliefs and values? 1 (Not at all important) – 5 (Extremely important)  
      
    
2. When learning a new skill, how important is it to you to be hands-on and learn by doing rather than just listening or reading about it? 1 (Not at all important) – 5 (Extremely important)  
      
    
3. How often do you take time for self-reflection and introspection to gain a deeper understanding of your thoughts and emotions? 1 (Never) – 5 (Very often)  
      
    
4. How important is it to you to have a sense of purpose and meaning in your life and work? 1 (Not at all important) – 5 (Extremely important)  
      
    
5. When working on a project, how much do you prefer to work independently or as part of a team? 1 (Strongly prefer to work independently) – 5 (Strongly prefer to work as part of a team)  
      
    
6. How important is it to you to have good communication skills, including being able to communicate within formal situations or institutions? 1 (Not at all important) – 5 (Extremely important)  
      
    
7. How interested are you in learning about different spiritual or philosophical beliefs and practices? 1 (Not at all interested) – 5 (Extremely interested)  
      
    
8. How much do you enjoy exploring your creativity and expressing yourself in different ways? 1 (Do not enjoy at all) – 5 (Enjoy very much)  
      
    
9. When faced with challenges or setbacks, how important is it to you to have a positive attitude and mindset? 1 (Not at all important) – 5 (Extremely important)  
      
    
10. How much do you value honesty, integrity, and ethical behavior in yourself and others? 1 (Do not value at all) – 5 (Value very much)  
      
    
11. How much do you enjoy building relationships with others and working collaboratively towards a common goal? 1 (Do not enjoy at all) – 5 (Enjoy very much)  
      
    
12. How important is it to you to have a well-thought-out plan and strategy for achieving success in your endeavors? 1 (Not at all important) – 5 (Extremely important)  
      
    

After completing the quiz and rating each question, the results can be used to determine the participant's preferences and strengths for each of the 12 learning styles. For example, if a participant rated questions 3, 6, and 9 as a 5, it may suggest that they have a higher preference for deep internal self-awareness, codified systems and productive communications, and emotional intelligence learning styles.

  

  

Interpretations from Quiz:

1. Personal Beliefs and Values: If a participant rates this question as a 4 or 5, it may suggest that they have a higher preference for learning styles that align with their personal beliefs and values.  
      
    
2. Hands-On Learning: If a participant rates this question as a 4 or 5, it may suggest that they have a higher preference for learning styles that involve hands-on activities and learning by doing.  
      
    
3. Self-Reflection: If a participant rates this question as a 4 or 5, it may suggest that they have a higher preference for learning styles that involve self-reflection and introspection.  
      
    
4. Purpose and Meaning: If a participant rates this question as a 4 or 5, it may suggest that they have a higher preference for learning styles that involve finding purpose and meaning in their work and life.  
      
    
5. Independent or Team Work: If a participant rates this question as a 4 or 5, it may suggest that they have a higher preference for learning styles that involve working either independently or as part of a team.  
      
    
6. Communication Skills: If a participant rates this question as a 4 or 5, it may suggest that they have a higher preference for learning styles that involve developing good communication skills, particularly in formal situations or institutions.  
      
    
7. Spiritual and Philosophical Beliefs: If a participant rates this question as a 4 or 5, it may suggest that they have a higher preference for learning styles that involve exploring different spiritual or philosophical beliefs and practices.  
      
    
8. Creativity: If a participant rates this question as a 4 or 5, it may suggest that they have a higher preference for learning styles that involve exploring their creativity and expressing themselves in different ways.  
      
    
9. Positive Attitude and Mindset: If a participant rates this question as a 4 or 5, it may suggest that they have a higher preference for learning styles that involve cultivating a positive attitude and mindset, particularly when facing challenges or setbacks.  
      
    
10. Honesty and Ethics: If a participant rates this question as a 4 or 5, it may suggest that they have a higher preference for learning styles that involve honesty, integrity, and ethical behavior, both in themselves and others.  
      
    
11. Interpersonal Relationships: If a participant rates this question as a 4 or 5, it may suggest that they have a higher preference for learning styles that involve building relationships with others and working collaboratively towards a common goal.  
      
    
12. Planning and Strategy: If a participant rates this question as a 4 or 5, it may suggest that they have a higher preference for learning styles that involve developing a well-thought-out plan and strategy for achieving success in their endeavors.
