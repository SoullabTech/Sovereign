Don Beck's Spiral Dynamics is a model of human development that identifies eight stages of consciousness or worldviews. These stages are:

1. Beige: The first stage is the survival mindset where individuals focus on meeting their basic needs such as food, water, and shelter.
2. Purple: The second stage is the tribal mindset, where individuals find safety in groups and follow the traditions and customs of their tribe or clan.
3. Red: The third stage is the power-oriented mindset where individuals seek power and control over others to fulfill their desires and impulses.
4. Blue: The fourth stage is the rule-oriented mindset where individuals seek order, structure, and stability by following the rules, laws, and traditions set by their society.
5. Orange: The fifth stage is the achievement-oriented mindset where individuals seek success, innovation, and progress through individual effort and merit.
6. Green: The sixth stage is the humanistic mindset where individuals seek to promote equality, diversity, and personal growth while challenging the status quo and traditional authority.
7. Yellow: The seventh stage is the integrative mindset where individuals seek to understand complex systems and develop new approaches to problem-solving that integrate multiple perspectives and worldviews.
8. Turquoise: The eighth stage is the holistic mindset where individuals seek to connect with the spiritual and cosmic dimensions of existence while promoting the well-being of all life on earth.

The four elements of consciousness refer to the levels of awareness identified by philosopher Ken Wilber, which are:

1. The "I" - individual consciousness
2. The "We" - collective consciousness
3. The "It" - objective consciousness
4. The "Its" - subjective consciousness

While there may be some similarities between these two models, there is no direct correlation between Don Beck's Spiral Dynamics and the four elements of consciousness. The Spiral Dynamics model emphasizes the evolution of individual and collective consciousness through different stages, while the four elements of consciousness focus on different aspects of awareness that can be present at any stage of development.
