### **Facilitator Guides: Step-by-Step Instructions for Using Spiralogic Templates**
#instructions #templates 
Below are detailed facilitator guides for leading sessions with the **Vision Mapping**, **Empathy Mapping**, and **Ripple Effect Mapping** templates. These guides include preparation steps, session structures, and tips for effective facilitation.

---

### **1. Vision Mapping Facilitator Guide (Fire Phase)**
#visionmapping #facilitatorguide 
#### **Purpose**:

- Help teams define a bold, unified vision and identify opportunities, challenges, and success metrics.

#### **Preparation**:

- **Materials**:
    - Vision Mapping Template (digital or printed).
    - Collaborative platform (Miro, MURAL, Jamboard) or whiteboard for in-person sessions.
- **Participants**:
    - Include all relevant team members to ensure diverse perspectives.

#### **Session Structure**:

1. **Introduction (5–10 Minutes)**:
    
    - Explain the purpose of Vision Mapping and how it aligns with team/project goals.
    - Example: _“Today, we’re going to create a shared vision for [project] and explore how we can achieve it together.”_
2. **Define the Central Goal (15 Minutes)**:
    
    - Ask: _“What is the bold, overarching objective of this project?”_
    - Collaboratively draft a central goal and write it in the middle of the template.
3. **Explore Opportunities (15 Minutes)**:
    
    - Ask: _“What creative approaches or methods can help us achieve this goal?”_
    - Encourage participants to share ideas and write them in the corresponding section.
4. **Identify Challenges (15 Minutes)**:
    
    - Ask: _“What obstacles might we encounter, and how can we overcome them?”_
    - Brainstorm challenges and potential solutions.
5. **Define Success Metrics (10 Minutes)**:
    
    - Ask: _“How will we measure success?”_
    - List specific, measurable outcomes (e.g., increased user engagement by 30%).
6. **Wrap-Up (5 Minutes)**:
    
    - Summarize the vision and key insights.
    - Example: _“Our vision is clear, and we’ve identified exciting opportunities, challenges, and ways to measure success.”_

#### **Tips for Facilitation**:

- Use prompts to spark discussion if participants hesitate.
- Encourage equal participation by inviting quieter team members to share.

### **2. Empathy Mapping Facilitator Guide (Water Phase)**
#EmpathyMapping 
#### **Purpose**:

- Foster emotional intelligence and trust by understanding team dynamics and user needs.

#### **Preparation**:

- **Materials**:
    - Empathy Mapping Template (digital or printed).
    - Sticky notes for in-person sessions or collaborative tools for digital use.

#### **Session Structure**:

1. **Introduction (5–10 Minutes)**:
    
    - Explain the importance of empathy in fostering collaboration and innovation.
    - Example: _“Today, we’ll explore our team’s emotions and needs to build stronger connections and support each other.”_
2. **Fill the Quadrants (30–40 Minutes)**:
    
    - **Feelings (10 Minutes)**:
        - Ask: _“What emotions are team members experiencing regarding this project?”_
        - Write responses in the “Feel” quadrant.
    - **What They Say (10 Minutes)**:
        - Ask: _“What concerns or ideas are team members expressing?”_
        - Capture phrases or recurring themes.
    - **What They Need (10 Minutes)**:
        - Ask: _“What do team members need to feel confident and supported?”_
        - List tangible and intangible needs.
    - **Actions to Take (10 Minutes)**:
        - Ask: _“What can we do as a team to address these needs and concerns?”_
        - Brainstorm actionable solutions.
3. **Wrap-Up (5 Minutes)**:
    
    - Highlight key themes and next steps.
    - Example: _“We’ve identified key emotions and needs, and now we can take specific actions to support our team.”_

#### **Tips for Facilitation**:

- Create a safe, nonjudgmental space for sharing.
- Use examples to help participants understand the exercise.

### **3. Ripple Effect Mapping Facilitator Guide (Aether Phase)**
#RippleEffectMapping 
#### **Purpose**:

- Visualize the immediate and long-term impacts of team actions to align efforts with the organization’s mission.

#### **Preparation**:

- **Materials**:
    - Ripple Effect Mapping Template (digital or printed).
    - Collaborative platform or physical chart paper for in-person sessions.

#### **Session Structure**:

1. **Introduction (5–10 Minutes)**:
    
    - Explain the concept of ripple effects and how they connect actions to larger impacts.
    - Example: _“Today, we’ll explore how our efforts create waves of impact, from immediate outcomes to long-term benefits.”_
2. **Fill the Template (30–40 Minutes)**:
    
    - **Core Actions (10 Minutes)**:
        - Ask: _“What specific actions are we taking now?”_
        - Write these in the innermost circle.
    - **Direct Impacts (10 Minutes)**:
        - Ask: _“What immediate outcomes will result from these actions?”_
        - Examples: _“Improved efficiency, positive user feedback.”_
    - **Long-Term Outcomes (10 Minutes)**:
        - Ask: _“How do these outcomes align with our values and mission?”_
        - Examples: _“Increased accessibility, stronger customer relationships.”_
    - **Reflect on Ripple Effects (10 Minutes)**:
        - Ask: _“What unexpected or secondary effects might these actions create?”_
        - Encourage creative thinking.
3. **Wrap-Up (5–10 Minutes)**:
    
    - Summarize the ripple map and reflect on its significance.
    - Example: _“Our actions today contribute to meaningful, long-lasting change.”_

#### **Tips for Facilitation**:
#facilitation 
- Encourage participants to think broadly and creatively about ripple effects.
- Use reflective prompts to deepen engagement.
