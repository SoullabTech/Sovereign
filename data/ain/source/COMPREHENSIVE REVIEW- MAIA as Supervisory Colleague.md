  **B. Crisis & Risk Protocols** **(Critical Gap)**

  

  **Current state**: Safety monitoring mentioned (line 47) but not detailed:

  6. Safety: Red flags (suicidality, dissociation, crisis)

  

  **What's needed**:

  - **What happens when MAIA detects acute risk?**

    - Does it alert practitioner immediately?

    - Does it trigger external notifications?

    - What's the protocol if practitioner doesn't respond?

  - **False positives** - MAIA flags crisis when none exists (how to handle?)

  - **False negatives** - MAIA misses crisis (liability question)

  - **Liability framework** - Who's responsible if MAIA fails to detect risk?

  

  **Recommendation**: Add section "Crisis Detection & Response Protocol":

  1. Risk assessment criteria MAIA uses

  2. Tiered alert system (yellow/orange/red)

  3. Practitioner response requirements

  4. Backup protocols (if practitioner unavailable)

  5. Liability disclaimers

  6. False positive/negative procedures

  **E. Limitations & Failure Modes** **(Critical Gap for Trust)**

  

  **Current state**: Mentions fallibility (lines 765-768):

  6. Fallibility Acknowledged

     - MAIA will be wrong sometimes

     - Algorithms have biases (as do humans)

     - MAIA's "confidence scores" are probabilities, not certainties

  

  **What's needed** - Much more specific:

  - **Known limitations**:

    - "MAIA cannot detect XYZ"

    - "MAIA's accuracy drops below 60% when..."

    - "MAIA requires minimum 20 minutes of audio to..."

  - **Common misreadings**:

    - "MAIA may misread grief as dorsal shutdown"

    - "MAIA may confuse therapeutic silence with dissociation"

  - **When to ignore MAIA**:

    - "If MAIA's suggestion contradicts your felt sense, trust yourself"

  - **What MAIA cannot do**:

    - Detect lying

    - Read micro-expressions (audio-only)

    - Understand complex trauma from single session

    - Replace live supervision

  

  **Recommendation**: Add section "MAIA's Limitations & When to Disregard":

  1. Technical limitations (what data MAIA needs, doesn't have)

  2. Known misreading patterns

  3. Confidence threshold guidelines (ignore suggestions below X confidence)

  4. Red flags that MAIA is misunderstanding client

  5. Practitioner override examples
