Summary of Vital Insights:

LIDA - A comprehensive cognitive architecture implementing Global Workspace Theory, featuring cognitive cycles that operate at ~10 Hz as the "atoms" of cognition [LIDA (cognitive architecture) - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LIDA_\(cognitive_architecture\)). It's particularly notable for its emphasis on consciousness as essential for learning and its practical applications in US Navy personnel systems.

SOAR - One of the oldest and most established cognitive architectures, based on the Problem Space Hypothesis where all goal-oriented behavior can be cast as search through a space of possible states while attempting to achieve a goal [Soar (cognitive architecture) - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soar_\(cognitive_architecture\)). It's been successfully deployed in NASA shuttle preparation and various game AI applications.

ACT-R - A hybrid cognitive architecture that combines symbolic and subsymbolic processing, with a theory that cognition is optimally adaptive, and precise estimates of cognitive functions mirror statistical properties of the environment [ACT-R - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ACT-R). It's particularly successful in educational applications with Cognitive Tutors used in thousands of schools.

POET - Not a cognitive architecture but an evolutionary algorithm that pairs the generation of environmental challenges and the optimization of agents to solve those challenges [POET: Endlessly Generating Increasingly Complex and Diverse Learning Environments and their Solutions through the Paired Open-Ended Trailblazer | Uber Blog](https://www.uber.com/blog/poet-open-ended-deep-learning/). Developed at Uber AI Labs, it represents a breakthrough in open-ended learning where both problems and solutions co-evolve.

MicroPsi (emphasized in your request) - A unique architecture based on Psi theory that tackles the problem of the interrelation of motivation and emotion with cognitive processes [MicroPsi: Contributions to a Broad Architecture of Cognition | SpringerLink](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-540-69912-5_2). It's distinguished by placing emotions and drives at the center of cognition, with both academic implementations and commercial applications in robotic vision.
