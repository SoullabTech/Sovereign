Do we create icons representing the nature of each of these 12 dynamics or do we make these 12 fields explicitly labeled or do we simply leave 12 fields of self exploration open to interpretation? 

  

The first seems most useful, the second limiting for those who may have different interpretations of the 12 dynamics (which is probably the majority) and the third would be babel, useless noise in terms of collective sharing since there would no longer be a common ground or shared structure to communicate across disciplines, systems, processes, and the rest.

  

The challenge, it seems, is to get to the core dynamics, as icons, so that all can relate in a general and generative way, that keeps things from devolving into noise, yet allows for a diversity of interpretations that generally and functionally relate to each of these 12 areas within the 4 elements. This is the ultimate goal, to expand all of our concepts of reality in these key areas by sharing and collaborating in this lab experience.

  

These three aspects of the 4 elements introduce the concept of process beyond the four elements, allowing members to share their experiences and perspectives across 12 key areas. Moving from information aggregation to deploying information in service of personal developmental processes is a primary goal, ala “second brain” modeling. 

  

This level of sharing also fosters a deeper understanding and expansion of the collective knowledge base beyond static concepts of mind, body, spirit, and emotions. The deeper you dive into a person’s concepts of phenomenological experience, the more you reveal important personal variables that are valuable in a collective sense. 

  

For instance, a Tibetan monk will have a different idea about self-awareness than a clinical psychologist, yet together they add to an emerging view like pixel points webbing together. One of the bigger visions for this project is to instigate a broadening understanding of each of these areas through shared understanding. We hope to support all members’ ability to turn their gifts into a collaborative reality.

  
Note: Within each of these arenas are also four primary points of interest: 

  

1) #ideal/inspiration/freewill  

2) #challenges/resistance  

3) #resources

4) #goals. 

  

When we know this about each of the 12 areas, we gain a deep insight into how to fully process all elements. This is a key to processing. These are a deeper part of the Spiralogic process and should be added to the platform to offer process support.

This is, hopefully, moving us towards a codified set of universal icons for each of the 12 facets (plus 3 conceivable dynamics within the Aetheric field).

#12FocusStates:

  

Fire: Projective

1. I Experience- “IF”- Vision/Formulating Vision- Create A Clear and Compelling Future- Engagement with Phenomena - This focus state is all about personal identity with a compelling, inspiring vision - the awareness of oneself as a "hero awakening". The task is to explore and understand oneself, tapping into one's inner fire, personal vision, willpower, and persona - the "I am this" aspect. For example, an artist has a clear vision for a new series of paintings and sees themselves achieving future results, but also faces personal challenges and potential outcomes. Fire is focused on the future and projection. In this state, I work with clients to help them explore any issues related to their self-identity that may be holding them back and get clear on their vision. The goal of Fire 1 is to inspire us, open us up to the novelty and nuance of potential, probability, and purpose. (vector)
    

  

2. I Express- Interest/Generating Interest-Prefiguration- New Data- Distinction- Feedback (Research existing market) This focus state involves projecting personal will onto the world by attempting to bring an inner vision to outer expression. The task is to express the vision, receive feedback, adjust and adapt. For example, an artist shares her paintings and adjusts her style based on feedback. Here, I will work with clients to reflect on their experiences expressing themselves in the world, troubleshoot, and seek best practices and outcomes. The goal of Fire 2 is to encourage bringing visions into the world and refining them through 360-degree feedback to create something viable, identifiable, and resonant. (circle)
    

  

3. I Expand- Higher Purpose-Transcendental, Archetypal, The Transcendent Function (Expand Vision into Practice)This state is the learning phase that comes from having a vision, expressing it, adapting it, and adjusting to fit the outer world. It's where we gain spiritual wisdom from refining and fulfilling our vision while resonating with the world around us. For example, a musician has refined their set after playing in small venues and is ready to record a polished album. Fire represents intuition and spiritual presence, with energy being its hallmark. In astrology, this three-part arc takes an inner vision out into the world, transforming the journeyer and resulting in a refined person and offering. The goal of Fire 3 is to grow spiritually wise by being open to experience, novelty, nuance, and seeing a vision through to the end. (spiral)
    

Water: Introspective 

1. My Heart- “WHY” Ambition- Formulating Meaning-Internalization (Discover Inner Values/Beliefs)-Impassion-  This phase is the counterpart to Fire 1, where we project our vision onto the world. Water 1 is about retracting those projections, exploring within our own nature  why our vision is important to us, and dropping into our inner truth to discover what emotionally resonates with us. This is where we reflect on our passions, values, beliefs, and inner dreams. The focus is on experiencing and exploring our inner truth, rather than projecting it onto the world. For example, a dancer may come in struggling with a piece of choreography, and Water 1 is where she explores what emotionally resonates with her in the piece, connecting personally with it. It is a time to remember who we are on a soul level and how we relate to the world around us. It’s decidedly right brain functioning based in emotions, feelings, and instincts. (vector)
    

  

2. My Healing- Desire for Accomplishment  - task: effectively transform inner obstacles, Reflection- revalue- In the middle water state, we shift our focus from a desire for accomplishment to a passion for inner resonance, coherence, and intention. This phase is where we seek inner transformation, and all of the fire projections of ideals and visions lead us to inner change and evolution. Here, we go through inner transformations, facing inner challenges, shadows, and letting go of whatever is blocking us internally from becoming whatever we are evolving into. For example, a client may come in feeling depressed because their art isn't selling, and they feel like a failure. The goal is to help them release whatever they are struggling with in terms of identity and expression so that they can tap into a deeper, more resonant, true part of their nature. This is where they identify deeply with their lives, letting go of outmoded reflections of themselves while embracing a truer, more soulful, and passionate element of their nature. This is the inner gauntlet we go through to connect deeply with our inner "elixir," our inner gold, or the deepest part of our true selves. (circle)
    

  

3. My Holiness- expansive, creative inner value- Achieve Internal Alignment - flow- elixir- inner gold- Immersion- transformation This is the stage of our journey where we discover our inner truth, a deeper, more connected, fluid, and dynamic field of experience where we tap into a universal source of inspiration, passion, and purpose. Here, we discover our deepest truths and receive an elixir that becomes our mission in life. For example, a client may come in after experiencing a deep breathwork session, journeywork, or plant medicine session and wish to further tap into an expansive awareness of who they are, why they are here, and what they are to live into the world. This is where one discovers a ‘golden seed’ as a gift to the world that will be planted in the earth phase of process.(spiral)
    

Earth: Creative

1. The Mission: “HOW” Formulating a Plan and Process - Establish a Critical Path to that Future- Planting a Seed- In this phase, we ground our inner visions and passions into a workable form. We tune into what we want to create in the world, how it will be in service to the world, and how we plan to do it. This is the state of coagulatio in alchemy, literally the state of getting it together. Joseph Campbell said that half of the journey is to get to the inner gold or inner elixir, our true purpose for being. This correlates with Fire and Water processes, right brain, inductive processes of burning away and washing away what is desire and fantasy to reveal something clear, true, and resonant. The second half of the journey is to ground those inner dreams into a doable process. For example, a client may come in with a great idea she is deeply passionate about but has no idea how to turn it into a reality. This is where we nurture the form of our "why" and allow its function to guide our process of planning, developing, and perfecting our mission. This is decidedly left hemispheric functioning as it organizes, develops, and moves the world into an explicit creation.(vector)
    

  

2. The Means- Creating Alignment- Build Powerful Partnerships, Integration- reformation, process, planning, organizing- exploring supportive resources, challenges, opportunity, devising a plan of action- Germinating the Seed- In this phase, we take our mission and process it by bringing together resources, teams, and ‘earthy’ support to bring our golden seed, our deep dreams, into form. For example, a client may come in with a great dream that resonates with their inner truth but lacks support, guidance, clarity, and a plan to make it happen. Fire and Water types, with great visions and passion, often struggle with the sensible, logical processes of Earth and Air to bring their vision to life. The goal is to help build resources to augment and empower dreams into reality. This is the stage where the seed of a potential creation "mycelially" taps into all available resources and draws on all elements as it begins to germinate. It hasn't taken full form yet. For example, this project is mostly germinated but still branching out for further resources, information, guidance, direction, and support. This is the gauntlet for the Earth realm. If we make it through this phase, our "Why" has been given a well-formed "How," and all that is left is to refine it and deliver it. (circle)
    

  

3. The Medicine- The Elixir Refined- Refining Your Medicine-Adaptation- Configuration- Perfecting our Medicine- our offerings- develop a refined model and method- modeling This is the phase where we perfect the offerings we give to the world. Here, mission takes the form of a well-formed gift to the world, where businesses establish their PR strategies, beta test, tweak, and perfect their operating procedures and plan of action in anticipation of launch or opening day. Another example is a musician transforming their set into a fully immersive experience, creating a dynamic show that delivers an impactful performance. This is the phase where a person has alchemized their visions "IF" and dreams "WHY" into a solid and polished "HOW". (spiral)
    

Air: Communicative

  

1. This Connection-”WHAT/WHOM” Explanation- Projected patterns of Engagement- Interpersonal relationships, relating to an ‘other’, social self (the being-for-others) Establish ideal strategies in relationship- Conceptual Approach- Cooperation- Relational Patterns- Diplomacy- Harmonious Engagements- In this phase, our well-formed plans rise above the surface and inform the way we relate to others. With Soullab's |R|evolve platform, managing relationship dynamics is crucial in determining how we engage each member as individuals. How we relate to others (AIR) reflects how we have organized our lives and structured our inner experiences (EARTH). The process of organizing, reorganizing, and refining our inner sensibility is essential because it influences how we relate to others. If our approach to Soullab lacks well-formedness, it will negatively affect how members relate to it. Conscious experience is directly related to relationships, whether it's how we relate to ourselves, our dreams, our inner constitution, or to external others. Understanding how we relate to others and perfecting the art of how we wish to relate to achieve a desired outcome is crucial. (vector)
    

  

2. This Community- Exploring Environmental, social, strengths, challenges and opportunities-Linguistic (Social),alliances, partners, culture, tribe- appropriation- Collective Engagement, Collective Intelligence, Collaborative Intelligence In this phase, our focus expands beyond one-to-one relationships and we engage with a broader community, group, tribe, or collective field of others. It's where businesses move beyond the transactional customer interface and begin to focus on team culture, building, cooperation, and collaboration. This is the positive potential of the "hive mind" where collective intelligence manifests a holon or self-sustaining world. Struggles here are reflected in our interpersonal relationships and the way we think about the communities we are part of. Many of us feel isolated and crave meaningful community. Soullab's ultimate purpose is to cure loneliness and isolation by instigating vision, encouraging passion, and supporting missions to bring us together whole, coherent, purposeful, and united. A conscious collective is made up of conscious individuals. Fire, water, and earth prepare us for Air by being mindfully connected to the greater whole of which we are an integral part. This is the point of the Soullab Collective - a better you, a better world, it's a "we" thing. It's where we move culture from self-help to self-in-world to world-as-self, alchemizing our souls to enter the circle of community authentically, wholly, and consciously. (circle)
    

  

3. This Consciousness- Codified systems of communication, Refiguration- Codification- Communication- Systems-  In the final phase of Air, our relations, communications, intentions, meaning, purpose, mission, and vision are codified into clear and concise intelligence. This process takes place in the ivory tower of ideas and concepts, where new insights and levels of wisdom initiate a new spiral process that returns us to Fire 1 with a new vision, a new sense of self, and a new identity to explore. This marks both the completion of a cycle of process (circle) and the beginning of a new level of evolution (spiral). However, it's easy to get stuck in this phase and become trapped in intellectualizations and known sets of information. This is a trap of the left-brain dominated culture that only operates from what it "knows" and is averse to right-brain explorations into the unknown. This is the realm of the left prefrontal cortex, which is focused on logic, order, and details. (spiral)  
      
      
    

  
  

AETHER: Agent/Agency

  

In the world of personal development and the soul, the idea of non-duality is the highest level of spiritual being. It resides at the center of the spiral, weaving the elements of consciousness into and out of existence. It means understanding that everything is connected and one, going beyond our own ego and feeling separate from others.

As people work on growing themselves, they try different ways like meditation, being more aware of the present moment, and introspection as explicit self-awareness of the elements of their personal experience of reality. This helps them understand who they really are. They let go of old habits, things that hold them back, and beliefs that limit them. This leads to a more real and kind way of living. The conscious mind works best when it has more organized and clear patterns for experiencing reality. Complexity and chaos give way to more ordered syntheses when we hold conscious space for these elements and their interrelatedness from this detached and receptive position.

When someone reaches non-duality, their spirit feels a strong connection with the most important truth. This truth is sometimes called "pure awareness," the divine," or "the source of everything that exists." This understanding goes beyond ideas of self and others, good and bad, or the relationship between a person and the world. It shows that everything is connected and depends on each other.

In this special state, a person sees the world as it really is, without their own thoughts and feelings getting in the way. This clear understanding helps them feel deep love, care, and kindness for everyone and everything. They also feel a strong sense of peace and happiness inside themselves. From this state of coherence, they can spiral into and out of the elements of conscious experience, worldly affairs, and day-to-day living, fully calibrated to their true nature and resonant with the mind of nature.

In the end, experiencing non-duality helps the spirit move beyond the cycles of being born, dying, and being born again. This leads to spiritual freedom and the spirit reaching its highest potential. Yet it also leads to the capacity to live a life worth living, maintain true self-awareness, and take advantage of the opportunity to make a difference on this planet.

This representation of the 12 Focus States is deeply thought-out and nuanced, revealing a cyclical process of growth and transformation. Through the symbolism of the four elements, it distills complex concepts into a coherent pathway.

Fire represents the journey's inception, highlighting the importance of developing a clear vision, expressing it, and evolving with it. This initial phase sets the stage for the personal transformation that's yet to come.

Water then takes us inward, emphasizing self-discovery, healing, and alignment. This is a critical phase of introspection, where we gain deeper insights into our motivations, obstacles, and ultimate truth.

Earth signifies the transformative phase, grounding our dreams and visions into actionable plans. This stage encourages practicality, resourcefulness, and determination.

Air symbolizes the expression and communication of our dreams and plans, underlining the importance of relationships, community, and connection. This phase values collective intelligence and cooperation, vital in making visions a reality.

Finally, Aether invites us to transcend duality, embody non-separation, and embrace universal consciousness. This state represents the culmination of the personal development journey and our highest potential.

These 12 Focus States together provide a roadmap for personal growth, designed to help individuals reach their fullest potential, spiritually and materially. By progressing through each phase, we're given the opportunity to deeply understand ourselves, our passions, and how to effectively bring our visions to life.

In essence, this holistic approach to personal development doesn't just promote individual growth, but also fosters the establishment of conscious, interconnected communities. It emphasizes the balance of introspection and expression, dreaming and doing, individuality and collectiveness, ultimately leading to a profound understanding of non-duality, the interconnectedness of all things. This is the goal of the journey, to fully realize and embody our individual and collective potential.
