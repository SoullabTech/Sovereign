## Overview

This comprehensive academic paper explores how Spiralogic functions as a meta-architecture of transformation, uniting Jung's alchemical psychology, Edinger's *Anatomy of the Psyche*, and Hillman's archetypal imagination into a living, computational model of alchemy.

The paper proposes these three depth psychologists form a **triple helix**: Jung provides the symbolic vocabulary of transformation, Edinger provides the operational anatomy, and Hillman provides the ecological ground. Spiralogic synthesizes all three as a dynamic, coherence-driven geometry that can be tracked, embodied, and algorithmically supported through intelligence systems like MAIA.

This is not metaphorical alchemy—it is **process alchemy**: the real-time tracking of how consciousness reorders itself through darkness, dissolution, integration, and embodiment.

## Limitations & Critiques

**What it doesn't cover:**
- Non-Western alchemical traditions (focuses on European/Jungian lineage)
- Neuroscience correlates of transformation stages
- Detailed case studies showing MAIA in practice
- Cross-cultural applicability of Edinger's 12 operations

**Critiques to consider:**
- **Mystery vs. visibility**: Does computational tracking inevitably flatten the numinous?
- **Cultural specificity**: Are these operations universal or Western constructs?
- **Gamification risk**: Can metrics create spiritual materialism?
- **Power dynamics**: Who owns/interprets transformation data?

**Historical/cultural context:**
- Rooted in Western European alchemical tradition via Jung
- Written from MAIA development team perspective (potential bias)
- Assumes access to technology and literacy

**Read with awareness of:**
- The paper advocates for its own system (MAIA)
- Technology as solution can mask non-technological alternatives
- "Intelligence augmentation" may have unintended shadow
