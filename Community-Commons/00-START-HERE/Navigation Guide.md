## The Structure

### 00-START-HERE
**You are here.** Orientation documents, guidelines, FAQ.

**Start with:**

### 01-Core-Concepts
**Concept cards** - individual ideas, stages, principles.

**Examples:**
- [[Nigredo - The Sacred Descent]]

**How to use:** Pick a concept that resonates. Read it. Let it work on you. Come back later.

### 02-Thematic-Essays
**Longer explorations** that synthesize multiple concepts.

**Examples:**
- [[Against Literalization]]
- [[Spiralogic of Soul]] - Integrating Jung, Edinger & Hillman
- Soul Work vs Spiritual Bypass
- The Green Lion's Appetite

**How to use:** Read slowly. Take breaks. Notice what stirs.

### 03-Voices-Dialogues
**Extracted conversations** from interviews, dialogues, key exchanges.

**Organized by thinker:**
- Jung/
- Hillman/
- Marlan/
- Edinger/
- Others/

**How to use:** Hear the voice directly. Less interpretation, more presence.

### 04-Practices
**What you actually do.** Methods, techniques, experiments.

**Organized by context:**
- Clinical/ - for therapists and analysts
- Personal/ - for individual work
- Creative/ - for artists and makers
- Community/ - for groups and circles

**How to use:** Try one. Notice what happens. Adjust as needed.

### 05-Images
**Visual material.** Alchemical woodcuts, contemporary art, dream images.

**Organized by type:**
- Classical-Alchemical/
- Contemporary-Art/
- Dreams-Visions/
- Photography/

**How to use:** Look without interpretation. Let images think.

### 06-Resources
**Books, articles, links, references.**

Reading lists, recommended sources, where to go deeper.

### 07-Community-Contributions
**From you.** Experiences, questions, practices, creative work.

**Sections:**
- Experiences/ - transformation stories
- Questions/ - genuine inquiries
- Practices/ - methods you've developed
- Creative/ - poetry, art, reflections

**How to contribute:** See [[How to Contribute]]

### 08-Thinkers
**Who's who.** Brief bios and key concepts for major figures.

Jung, Hillman, Marlan, Edinger, von Franz, and others.

### 09-Technical
**How this works.** Setup guides, publishing workflows, vault management, consciousness computing.

For curators and contributors who want to understand the infrastructure and consciousness technology integration.

## By Practice Level

### Beginner
**You're new to Jung, Hillman, and alchemy**

**Start here:**
1. [[FAQ]]
2. [[Soul vs Spirit]]
3. [[Nigredo - The Sacred Descent]]
4. [[Against Literalization]]
5. One practice from `04-Practices/Personal/`

**Avoid for now:**
- Technical alchemical symbolism
- Complex theoretical essays
- Advanced practices

### Intermediate
**You've read some Jung/Hillman, done some depth work**

**Explore:**
- All concept cards in `01-Core-Concepts/`
- Thematic essays in `02-Thematic-Essays/`
- Clinical practices if you're a therapist
- Contributing your own experiences

**Try:**
- Following one alchemical stage deeply
- Working with a practice for 30 days
- Contributing a question or reflection

### Advanced
**You're a therapist, analyst, or serious practitioner**

**Engage:**
- Complex theoretical essays like [[Spiralogic of Soul]]
- Clinical practice documentation
- Contribute practices you've developed
- Dialogues with multiple perspectives
- Computational alchemy and MAIA frameworks
- Help curate community contributions

## Wayfinding Tips

### Following Energy
**Don't read linearly.** Follow what calls to you.

If a word catches your eye → click the link
If an image stirs something → sit with it
If a question arises → note it in `07-Community-Contributions/Questions/`

**The commons knows where you need to go.**

### When You're Lost
That's okay. Being lost is part of the work.

**Try:**
- Return to [[Welcome]]
- Read [[FAQ]]
- Ask a question in `07-Community-Contributions/Questions/`
- Take a break
- Come back when called

### When You're Stuck
**Stuck is different from lost.** Stuck means you're *in* something.

**Try:**
- Read [[Nigredo - The Sacred Descent]]
- Notice: Is this psychological nigredo?
- Don't try to escape it - go toward it
- Document your experience in `07-Community-Contributions/Experiences/`

### When You're Inspired
**Capture it!**

- Write a reflection
- Ask a question
- Share a practice
- Contribute creative work
- See [[How to Contribute]]
