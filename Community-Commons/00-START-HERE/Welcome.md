## Why Alchemy & Psychology?

Carl Jung discovered that medieval alchemical texts, which seemed to be about transforming lead into gold, were actually describing psychological transformation processes. The alchemists were unknowingly projecting the individuation process onto their work with substances.

This gave psychology a rich symbolic library:
- **Nigredo** (blackening) - depression, descent, shadow work
- **Albedo** (whitening) - clarity, insight, purification
- **Citrinitas** (yellowing) - embodiment, bringing insights to earth
- **Rubedo** (reddening) - full integration, life force, incarnation

These aren't just interesting ideas - they're **maps for transformation** that people have found useful for navigating their own psychological and spiritual journeys.

James Hillman developed this further through **archetypal psychology**, emphasizing:
- Working with images rather than concepts
- Soul over spirit (depth over transcendence)
- Staying with the image
- Honoring pathology as soul-speech
- Against literalization

Stanton Marlan and others continue this work, exploring the depths of alchemical psychology in contemporary practice.
