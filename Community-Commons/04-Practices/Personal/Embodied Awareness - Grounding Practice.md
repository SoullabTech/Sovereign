# Embodied Awareness: Grounding in the Body

*Bringing consciousness into flesh - the foundation of soul work*

---

## What It Is

**Embodied awareness** is the practice of bringing conscious attention into your physical body - feeling, sensing, and inhabiting your flesh rather than living primarily in your head.

**Grounding** is anchoring yourself in physical reality - earth, gravity, sensation - particularly when you're floating in abstraction, dissociation, or overwhelm.

**This is citrinitas work** - yellowing the whiteness, bringing spirit into matter.

See [[Citrinitas - The Golden Dawn]]

---

## Why This Matters

**Most depth work can become too "heady":**
- Dream analysis → All mental
- Shadow work → Intellectualized
- Spiritual practice → Dissociated from body
- Therapy → Talk with no feeling

**Without embodiment:**
- Insights don't integrate
- Understanding stays abstract
- Transformation incomplete
- You're split (mind vs body)

**With embodiment:**
- Insights land in flesh
- Understanding becomes felt knowing
- Transformation completes
- You're whole

**The body is where the work completes.**

**Jung:** "The Self wants to be made flesh."

**Hillman:** "Soul work requires body."

---

## When to Use This

**Use grounding practices when:**

**1. You're too much in your head**
- Overthinking, analyzing, spinning
- Disconnected from feeling
- Floating in abstraction

**2. Overwhelmed by emotions**
- Anxiety, panic
- Rage, grief (too much)
- Need to regulate

**3. After intense inner work**
- Active Imagination session
- Shadow work
- Big dream
- Therapy session

**4. Dissociated or "spacey"**
- Trauma response
- Checking out
- Not present

**5. Before important action**
- Difficult conversation
- Creative work
- Decision-making
- Need to be centered

**6. Daily practice**
- Morning grounding
- Evening settling
- Throughout day check-ins

## Basic Grounding: The 5-Minute Practice

**Can be done anywhere, anytime:**

### Step 1: Feel Your Feet

**Sitting or standing:**
- Bring attention to your feet
- Feel them touching ground/floor
- Notice the contact points
- Press gently into earth

**Say internally:** "I am here. My feet are on the ground."

### Step 2: Feel Gravity

- Notice your weight
- Feel yourself pulled toward earth
- Let yourself be heavy
- Surrender to gravity

**Don't fight it. Let earth hold you.**

### Step 3: Breath (Belly, not chest)

- Hand on belly
- Breathe deep into abdomen
- Feel belly rise and fall
- Slow, full breaths (4 count in, 6 count out)

**3-5 breaths minimum**

### Step 4: Body Scan (Quick)

**Head to toe, notice:**
- Tension? Where?
- Temperature? Hot/cold?
- Sensation? Tingling, numbness, pain?

**Just notice. Don't fix.**

### Step 5: Ground Your Gaze

**Open eyes (if closed):**
- Look around the room
- Name 5 things you see
- Touch something textured
- Engage physical senses

**This brings you back to here/now.**

**Total time: 5 minutes or less**

**Result: More present, more grounded, more embodied**

## Full Embodiment Practice (30 min)

**For deeper grounding:**

### Part 1: Arrival (5 min)

**Find a comfortable position** (sitting, lying, standing)

**Close eyes or soft gaze**

**Notice:**
- Where am I right now? (physically, emotionally, mentally)
- How did I arrive here?
- What am I carrying?

**Acknowledge** without judgment

### Part 2: Breath (5 min)

**Deep belly breathing:**
- Hand on belly
- Inhale: Belly expands
- Exhale: Belly softens

**Count:**
- Inhale: 4 counts
- Hold: 2 counts
- Exhale: 6 counts
- Hold: 2 counts

**Repeat 10-20 cycles**

**This regulates nervous system**

### Part 3: Body Scan (10 min)

**Slowly scan from feet to head:**

**Feet:**
- Feel toes, soles, heels
- Any sensation?
- Can you soften them?

**Legs:**
- Calves, knees, thighs
- Notice tension or ease

**Pelvis/Hips:**
- Sit bones, hips, lower belly
- Seat of instinct and sexuality
- Can you breathe into this area?

**Belly/Solar Plexus:**
- Gut, diaphragm
- Emotional center
- What do you feel here?

**Chest/Heart:**
- Ribcage, lungs, heart
- Notice the beat, the breath
- Any tightness or opening?

**Shoulders/Arms:**
- Often hold tension
- Can you drop them?
- Let arms be heavy

**Neck/Throat:**
- Swallowing, speaking
- Any constriction?
- Soften

**Face/Head:**
- Jaw (often clenched)
- Eyes, forehead
- Soften face
- Let head be heavy

**Whole body:**
- Sense it all at once
- One living, breathing system
- You ARE this

### Part 4: Earth Connection (5 min)

**Feel your connection to earth:**

**If sitting:**
- Feel sit bones on chair
- Feet on floor
- Let gravity take you down

**If lying:**
- Feel back, legs, head touching surface
- Let earth support you completely
- You don't have to hold yourself up

**Visualize (optional):**
- Roots from your tailbone into earth
- Drawing up earth energy
- Anchoring you

### Part 5: Integration (5 min)

**Ask:**
- What does my body know right now?
- What is it asking for?
- What needs to be honored?

**Listen** for body wisdom (not mental answers)

**Give thanks** to your body for holding you

**Slowly return**:
- Wiggle fingers, toes
- Stretch gently
- Open eyes
- Take your time standing

## Practices for Specific Needs

### When Anxious/Overwhelmed

**The 5-4-3-2-1 Technique**

**Name out loud:**
- **5 things** you can SEE
- **4 things** you can TOUCH (and touch them)
- **3 things** you can HEAR
- **2 things** you can SMELL
- **1 thing** you can TASTE

**This anchors you in sensory present**

**Plus:**
- Feet on ground
- Deep belly breaths
- "I am safe. I am here."

### When Dissociated

**Strong sensory input:**

- **Cold water** on face/hands
- **Ice cube** in hand
- **Strong scent** (peppermint oil, coffee)
- **Textured object** (rough stone, soft fabric)
- **Loud sound** or **music**
- **Movement** (jumping jacks, shaking)

**Goal: Bring yourself back into body**

### When "Too Spiritual"

**If you're floating in transcendent states:**

**Earthing practices:**
- **Garden** - hands in soil
- **Cook** - engage food, smells, tastes
- **Walk barefoot** - on grass, earth, sand
- **Eat** - mindfully, slowly
- **Physical work** - clean, build, create with hands

**See [[Citrinitas - The Golden Dawn]]** - yellowing the whiteness

### After Intense Inner Work

**Active Imagination, shadow work, or therapy:**

**Immediately after:**
- Stand up
- Feel feet
- Walk around
- Touch physical objects
- Drink water
- Eat something (grounding food)

**Don't jump right back to tasks**

**Give body time to integrate**

## Movement Practices

**Embodiment through motion:**

### Shaking/Tremoring

**Animals do this to release trauma**

**Method:**
- Stand, feet shoulder-width
- Begin shaking gently
- Let it build organically
- Shake arms, legs, torso
- Let sound come if it wants
- 5-10 minutes
- Slow down gradually

**This releases stuck energy**

### Authentic Movement

**Intuitive, unplanned movement:**

- **Quiet space, alone or with witness**
- **Close eyes**
- **Wait for impulse to move**
- **Follow the impulse** (don't plan or choreograph)
- **Let body lead**
- **10-30 minutes**

**Unconscious material can emerge through movement**

### Walking Meditation

**Slow, mindful walking:**
- **Each step felt fully**
- **Heel, ball, toes**
- **Weight shifting**
- **Arms swinging naturally**
- **Breath coordinating with steps**

**Can be done outside (ideal) or inside**

**Not walking to get somewhere - walking to walk**

### Yoga/Qigong/Tai Chi

**Structured embodiment practices:**

**Benefits:**
- Unite breath and movement
- Build body awareness
- Cultivate qi/prana (life force)
- Ground and center

**Find a practice that resonates**

## Embodiment in Daily Life

**Not just formal practice - live embodied:**

### Eating

**Instead of:** Mindless, distracted eating

**Practice:**
- Taste each bite
- Notice texture, temperature
- Chew slowly
- Feel hunger and satiety

### Showering/Bathing

**Instead of:** Automatic routine

**Practice:**
- Feel water on skin
- Notice temperature
- Smell soap/shampoo
- Honor the body being tended

### Walking

**Instead of:** Head down, thinking

**Practice:**
- Feel each step
- Notice surroundings
- Breathe fresh air
- Be where you are

### Working

**Every hour:**
- Pause
- Feel feet
- 3 breaths
- Body check-in

**This prevents dissociation into work**

## Body as Oracle

**Your body knows things your mind doesn't:**

### Somatic Intuition

**Practice asking your body:**

**"Should I do X?"**

**Then feel:**
- Expansion or contraction?
- Ease or tension?
- Warmth or cold?
- Yes or no in the gut?

**Body wisdom precedes mental knowing**

**Trust it**

### Symptoms as Communication

**Body symptoms can be psyche speaking:**

**Headache:** What thoughts are too much?
**Jaw clenching:** What are you not saying?
**Stomach pain:** What can't you digest?
**Back pain:** What burden are you carrying?

**Not always symbolic - sometimes just physical**

**But worth asking**

## Working with Trauma

**If you have trauma history:**

**Important:**
- Embodiment can be triggering
- Body holds trauma
- Go slow
- Get support

**Trauma-informed practices:**
- **Choice** - always optional
- **Titration** - small doses
- **Pendulation** - between ease and difficulty
- **Support** - therapist, guide

**Resources:**
- Peter Levine - *Waking the Tiger*
- Bessel van der Kolk - *The Body Keeps the Score*
- Somatic Experiencing practitioners

**Don't force embodiment if body says no**
