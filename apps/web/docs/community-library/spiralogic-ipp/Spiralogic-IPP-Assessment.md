# Spiralogic-IPP Attachment Assessment

## Essence

A diagnostic tool that identifies missing Ideal Parent Functions and elemental imbalances using the integrated framework of Dan Brown & David Elliott's IPP research with Spiralogic's five-element system - providing precise targeting for attachment repair work.

## Description

The Spiralogic-IPP Assessment represents a breakthrough in attachment diagnosis, combining clinical precision with archetypal wisdom. By mapping Dan Brown & David Elliott's **14 Ideal Parent Functions** across **5 Spiralogic Elements**, this assessment identifies exactly which internal parent functions are missing and where attachment development stalled.

**Clinical foundation:** This assessment is grounded in decades of attachment research, particularly Brown & Elliott's Three Pillars Model and their discovery that specific parenting functions can be neuroplastically installed through targeted imagery work.

**Elemental innovation:** The five elements (Earth, Water, Fire, Air, Aether) provide archetypal containers that make attachment deficits more accessible to both client and therapist, creating clear pathways for corrective experience.

## Alchemical Context

The assessment embodies the alchemical principle of **Solve** - dissolving unconscious attachment patterns by bringing them into conscious awareness. Through accurate diagnosis, we create the foundation for **Coagula** - the reconstruction of secure internal working models.

The five elemental domains represent the complete spectrum of human developmental needs, each requiring different archetypal energies from caregivers. When these are missing, specific attachment wounds and compensatory patterns emerge.

### Scoring and Interpretation

#### **Elemental Deficit Levels:**
- **0-12:** Severe deficit - Primary treatment focus
- **13-20:** Moderate deficit - Secondary treatment target
- **21-26:** Adequate but fragile - Strengthening work
- **27-32:** Strong internalization - Resource for other elements

1. **Treatment Planning:** Lowest scores identify primary IPP imagery targets

3. **Progress Tracking:** Re-assessment shows internalization of ideal parent functions
