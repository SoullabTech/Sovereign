# Spiralogic-IPP Attachment Assessment Survey

*Diagnostic tool for identifying missing Ideal Parent Functions and elemental imbalances*

---

## Instructions for Use

This assessment identifies which Ideal Parent Functions were missing in your early development (ages 0-18) and maps them to the five elemental archetypal parents. Your results will guide targeted attachment repair work using the Spiralogic-IPP protocol.

### **How to Score:**

Rate each statement based on your **childhood experience (ages 0-18):**

- **0 = Never experienced** - This was completely absent
- **1 = Rarely experienced** - This happened occasionally
- **2 = Sometimes experienced** - This happened inconsistently
- **3 = Often experienced** - This happened regularly
- **4 = Consistently experienced** - This was reliable and predictable

**Important:** Answer based on what you actually experienced, not what you wish had happened or what your parents intended. Trust your body's memory of feeling states.

## Treatment Planning Guide

### **Step 1: Identify Primary Deficit**
Your **lowest scoring element** is your primary treatment focus. This represents the most essential missing Ideal Parent Function that needs installation through guided imagery work.

### **Step 2: Secondary Deficits**
Elements scoring **13-20** are secondary priorities. These will be addressed after the primary deficit shows improvement (usually 4-8 weeks of consistent imagery work).

### **Step 3: Integration Planning**
Elements scoring **21-26** may need strengthening work once severe deficits are addressed. High-scoring elements (**27-32**) become resources for supporting the healing of deficit areas.

### **Step 4: Cultural and Personal Adaptation**
Consider how your cultural background, family system, and unique circumstances may have influenced these patterns. The elemental framework can be adapted to honor diverse cultural expressions while addressing universal developmental needs.

## Cautions and Ethical Considerations

### **Not a Substitute for Professional Help:**
This assessment is a tool for understanding attachment patterns, not a replacement for professional mental health care. If you have:
- Active trauma symptoms
- Suicidal thoughts
- Substance abuse issues
- Serious mental health conditions

Please work with qualified professionals who can provide appropriate support alongside IPP work.

### **Re-Parenting vs. Parent Blame:**
This assessment is about **what you needed**, not about blaming your actual parents. Most parents did their best with their own resources and healing. The goal is installing what was missing, not judging what was provided.

### **Cultural Sensitivity:**
Parenting practices vary widely across cultures. Some variations in scoring may reflect cultural differences rather than deficits. Consider working with culturally informed practitioners who can help distinguish between cultural adaptation needs and universal attachment requirements.
